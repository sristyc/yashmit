SIX AND OUT
POEM
The pitch was only smooth in parts;
It sank at either crease,
And motor vans and bakers’ carts
At times disturbed the peace.
The bowlers found it hard to hit
The lamp post’s slender stem
The broader wicket opposite,
Was cleared at 6:00 pm
It was a keen, determined school,
Unorthodox and free;
Harsh circumstances oft made the rule,
And not the M.C.C.
The scorer, seated by the well,
Kept up a fire of talk;
He was both umpires, crowd and all,
And plied a busy chalk.
So, standing musing on the scene,
I let the moments pass;
How well he drove it to the screen.
And then- the crash of glass.
I watched the players as they ran,
And heard, while yet they fled,
The loud voice of an angry man,
The law’s majestic tread.
GD Martineau
About the author:
GD Martineau (1897-1976) was an English cricket writer. He wrote about different aspects of the
game of cricket. His notable works include Bat, Ball, Wicket and All, They made cricket, the
valiant Stumper and The Field Is Full of Shades. He died in England at the age of 79.
Summary of the poem:
Physicalgames have always been fascination of boys and girls everywhere in the world. Although
sports and physical games have been reduced to one’s living room due to scarcity of playground in
cities, Children in villages are luckier because they have space to play and enjoy. The poem presents
a similar situation where children have been forced to play cricket outside the park because it closed
down at 6 and they were Out! The children’s determination shows that space is not a limiting factor for
sports. All we need is determination and a keen interest to play. In the poem the children, were playing
on the road, are disturbed by the motor vans and the bakers’ cart. The road was narrow; they had to
use the base of lamp post as wicket and the wall of the well as score board. The wicket keeper scorer
is both, the umpire and the crowd. Yet they are determined to play out there and not bound by the
rules of M.C.C. (Marylebone Cricket Club).
The bowler hits the ball so hard that it goes and strikes the window pane or the screen of any vehicle
and broke its glass. The man of the house or may be the vehicle owner shouted and the children fled
from there.
Word meanings:
1. Pitch-an area painted with lines for playing particular sports, like cricket, football
2. Crease-the white line on the ground near each wicket where the batsman and the bowler
stand
3. school- (here) group
4. Unorthodox- different from usual
5. Fire of talk-(here) commentary
6. Plied- used skilfully
7. Musing- thinking
8. Law’s majesty- policeman
Analysis of the poem:
Opening stanza:
The pitch was only smooth in parts;
It sank at either crease,
And motor vans and bakers’ carts
At times disturbed the peace.
Paraphrase:
Theywere playing cricket in the street. The pitch was not entirely smooth. Both the pitches (where the
ball bounces when bowled) on both sides. Except the motor vans and bakers’ carts that sold bakery,
the game was altogether smooth.
Stanza-2
The bowlers found it hard to hit
The lamp post’s slender stem
The broader wicket opposite,
Was cleared at 6:00 pm
Paraphrase:
It was hard for the bowlers to hit the wicket because it was not really a wicket. They used the slender
base of the lamp post as a wicket and therefore it was hard to hit the wicket as it was not broad
enough. The broader wicket suggests that the road was opposite a park where there was a typical
cricket pitch with broader wicket.
Stanza-3
It was a keen, determined school,
Unorthodox and free;
Harsh circumstances oft made the rule,
And not the M.C.C.
Paraphrase:
The children were deeply interested in cricket. They did not follow the rules of cricket. They were free
thinkers and not bound by any rules and regulations needed to play the game. The harsh
circumstances were the tough and disturbing situations under which they were playing. The M.C.C
refers to the Marylebone Cricket Club, which was founded in 1787 and was the governing body of
cricket in England., Scotland and Wales.
Stanza-4
The scorer, seated by the well,
Kept up a fire of talk;
He was both umpires, crowd and all,
And plied a busy chalk.
Paraphrase:
The score was written by a boy who sat by the well. He was an all-rounder-umpire, commentator and
crowd. He kept on giving a commentary of the game. With the chalk in his hand, he kept on writing the
score.
Stanza-5
So, standing musing on the scene,
I let the moments pass;
How well he drove it to the screen.
And then- the crash of glass.
Paraphrase:
The narrator is ‘I’ in the stanza. The narrator was watching and thinking about the boys who were
playing the game of cricket. One of the batsman hit the ball very hard and the ball flew to a nearby
window and smashed the glass.
Stanza-6
I watched the players as they ran,
And heard, while yet they fled,
The loud voice of an angry man,
The law’s majestic tread.
Paraphrase:
The players ran away because one of them drove the ball into the nearby house and broke its window.
It can also refer to any vehicles front screen as they were playing on a busy road and the vehicles
were moving. The narrator heard the loud shout of the owner of the house or a vehicle whose screen
or the windowpane had broken. Law’s majestic tread` refers to the policeman and the fear that caught
the children after breaking the glass or screen, and their running away from the road because of the
fear of being caught. Law is majestic and violating the law evokes fear in the minds of people.

Ans: According to the speaker the pitch was not entirely smooth. It was uneven as it was a street and
not a regular cricket pitch, and it sank at both the crease or the surface was slightly lower than the rest.
Q2. What disturbed the peace?
Ans: The motor vans and bakers’ carts that sold bakery disturbed the peace or they were interrupting
the natural flow of the game.
Q3. What was used as the wicket and why did the bowlers find it hard to hit it?
Ans: It was hard for the bowlers to hit the wicket because it was not really a regular wicket. They used
the slender base of the lamp post as a wicket and therefore it was hard to hit the wicket as it was not
broad enough.
Q4. Where did the scorer sit and what did he do?
Ans. The scorer was seated beside the well. He was playing the role of a commentator, an umpire and
the crowd. He was busy maintaining the scoreboard with the application of a chalk.
Q5. Where the speaker was and what was he doing?
Ans: The narrator was watching the scene and thinking about the boys who were playing the game of
cricket on the busy street.

INTRODUCTION:
Yarn to Fabric
There are two major ways using which yarn is converted to fabric, namely, Weaving and Knitting.
 Weaving: The process of entwining two sets of yarn simultaneously to make fabric is called Weaving. The
process is done using a loom (which can either be operated by hand or by a machine) which interlaces two
sets of yarn at right angles to each other.
 Knitting: Knitting is the process by which a single strand of yarn is used to make a piece of fabric. Socks,
sweaters, mufflers and a lot of other winter clothes are made of knitted fabrics. Knitting can be done by
handas well as by machines.
History of Clothing Material
 In earlier times, when people did not have access or the knowledge to process fibre, big leaves and the bark
of trees were used by people to cover themselves
 After settlement began in agricultural communities, they learnt how to weave. They used grass and twigs to
make mats and baskets. Animal hair or fleece and vines were warped together into stretched out strands
which were then woven into fabrics.
 There was an abundant growth of cotton in areas near Ganga, which the early Indians readily used to make
fabrics for themselves.
 There is another plant named flax which yields natural fibres.
 The early Egyptians cultivated both cotton and flax and used them for creating fabrics. These plants grew
near the river Nile.
 But in those days, people were not aware of the process of stitching. They simply used to wrap around the
fabric around different parts of their bodies. Even today unstitched clothes like sarees, dhotis, lungis or
turbans are widely in use.
 It was with the advent of the sewing needle that people learnt how to stitch fibres to make fabric.

INTRODUCTION
Every country needs a government to take decisions. The government
provides all facilities to the citizens. Like electricity, water supply,
building schools, roads, hospitals, running postal and railway services
and several programmes to help the poor.
The government also protects the boundaries of the country and
maintains peaceful relations with other countries. In times of natural
disasters like Tsunami, Earthquake it organizes camps to aid and assist
the affected people. Courts also play a part of the government. If there is
a dispute or someone has committed a crime then he will be punished
by the courts. The government control resources and protects the
territory of a country.
LEVELS OF GOVERNMENT
The government works at three levels
1) Local level
2) State level
3) National level.
1) Local level: The local level means in the village there ia a Panchayat,
in towns there are municipalities and in cities there is a municipal
corporation.
2) State level: At the state level there is a state government in every
state.
3) National level: The national level there is the central government for
the entire country.
The three organs of the government are Legislature, Executive and the
Judiciary. The Legislature makes the laws, the Executive implements
the laws and the Judiciary checks the validity of the laws.
LAWS AND THE GOVERNMENT
The government makes laws and all the people have to abide by the
laws of the government. For example there is a law says that all persons
must have a license while driving a vehicle and if any person is caught
without a license then he will be jailed or fined with a large sum of
money.
TYPES OF GOVERNMENT
India has a democratic form of government and in a democracy the
people give the government the power to rule and promote the welfare
of the people. The government is responsible for all its actions to the
people as the government is formed by the elected representatives of the
people.
Another form of government is Monarchy. The monarch (King or
Queen) has the power to make decisions and run the government. The
monarch is not responsible for his decisions to the people. His decision

DEMOCRATIC GOVERNMENTS
India is a democracy and this achievement has come on its way after a
long eventful struggle of the Indian people. Democracy is the rule of the
majority elected by the people through voting. Such governments are
referred as Representative Democracies. In such governments people do
not participate directly but choose the representatives through election.
The government formed makes decisions for the entire population.
Universal Adult Franchise is the basic feature of a democratic
government. The right to vote of every adult citizen without any
discrimination on the basis of caste, creed, religion, social economic
background. There was also a time when women and poor were not
allowed to participate in elections. Only men who were educated and
owned property could vote. American women got the right to vote
in1920 and Britain got the right to vote in1928. During the First World
War women’s struggle to vote strengthened and this movement was
called Women’s Suffrage movement.
During the war in both America and European countries men were
fighting wars so women were called to do the work which was
considered as men’s work. Women began to organize and manage
different kind of work and people began to wonder that women are
equally capable to make decisions as men. Finally after a long drawn
struggle women got the right to vote.
Mahatma Gandhi in his journal “young India “explained the importance
of Universal Adult Franchise

TUTORIALS:-
Windows 7 Taskbar
 The Windows 7 taskbar is more flexible and provides an easy navigation
facility.
 We can pin our favourite programs anywhere on the taskbar for easy access.
 We can preview the thumbnails of the open files or programs by pointing to
their icons on the taskbar. To preview in full screen mode, simply move the
pointer over the thumbnail. Windows can even be closed from the thumbnail
preview.
 The new Aero Peek feature allows us to turn the open windows transparent
and makes the desktop screen visible. To do so, just point to the show desktop
button at the right edge of the taskbar.
Snap
 Drag one of the windows to the left edge of the screen and another to the right
edge. In this manner, we can view two windows side by side.
 Dragging the window to the upper edge of the screen will take up the entire
screen.
 Dragging the window to the lower edge of the screen will expand it vertically
occupying the half screen.
Shake
Click on the top of a window and without releasing the mouse button give your
mouse a shake. Every open window , except the one you are working with will be
minimised.
Windows media center
Watching TV, movies, and other video content on our PC is easier than ever. With a
cable connection, we can watch and record TV shows using Windows Media Center.
Jump List
This feature allows us to view the pinned documents, pictures, songs, Or websites that are
used frequently. To open a jump list, just right click on a program icon on the windows 7
taskbar. It will display a list of recently modified documents within that program. For
example, the jump list for Internet Explorer shows recently opened websites.
Aero flip 3D
This feature enables us to preview all open windows as a stack. To activate this feature,
press windows key + tab key combination on the keyboard.
